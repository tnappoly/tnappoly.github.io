Hi, I'm Tarun!

My passion for data science stems from an admiration for innovation that was fostered in Cupertino, California, where I spent most of my younger years, and fortunately still blessed to call home. I was always fascinated by all of the cutting-edge technologies being developed in this area and also the rapid pace at which they emerged. To me, it seemed like a perfectly orchestrated play with each business serving its role and contributing to the massive tech boom that we, who grew up here, got to witness first-hand. That fluid structure that businesses interacted with one another inspired me to pursue a degree in business and economics, but I knew that innovation through developing new technologies was where my heart was truly at. After, graduating college I enrolled in a data science bootcamp through General Assembly where I was able to study and practice topics that enthralled me, such as, supervised/unsupervised learning, neural networks, modeling for forecasting & classifying, and overall the idea of machine learning and artificial intelligence. Now, I am currently open to opportunities where I can apply my analytical skills and technical expertise to help companies innovate and bring their vision of the future to the present.

Mask Detection

Data science for social good project. Computer vision to identify whether people are wearing masks correctly. The essential premise of this experiment was to use machine learning to create a distinction between three classifications. These would serve as a means to support a strategic integration with both camera system working in stills, as well as, in motion to diagnose our particular classes.

MBTI Predictor

The Myers–Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) is a kind of psychological classification about human experience using four principal psychological functions, sensation, intuition, feeling, and thinking, constructed by Katharine Cook Briggs and her daughter Isabel Briggs Myers. This project showcases the use of Natural Language Processing (NLP) with classification models from the scikit-learn library in identifying which personality type from the Myers-Briggs personality types classifier a given person is, based on their own writing.

Real Estate Valuator -- Completion by 05/28/2021

Here, I set out to forecast house prices using regression models from the scikit-learn library. This project was used as a submission for a competition hosted on Kaggle to see who could construct the 'best model' to predict a house price in Ames, Iowa. The metric used for evaluation was Mean Squared Error. My best model had a mse of ±43,607 in USD.
